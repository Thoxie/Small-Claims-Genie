When you go to the Clerk of Courts to file your case, ask the clerk to send the defendant the claim by certified mail. The clerk will send the papers. Some courts require a local request form and a small certified-mail fee, so the user should ask the clerk whether anything else is needed before leaving the filing window. 
California court guidance states that clerk-certified-mail service is only offered in some courts, and county instructions often tell users to check back before the hearing to see whether the signed receipt was returned. 
Important:
Do not assume the defendant has been served just because the clerk mailed the papers. Service is only complete if the certified-mail process is signed by the defendant. produces acceptable proof for the court. The user should check the court file on line with the Case Number or contact the clerk to confirm whether the signed receipt came back and whether the court accepted it. Check with the court about 10 to 14 days after filing.
If the defendant does not sign, refuses delivery, ignores the postal notice, someone else signs in a way the court does not accept, or the mail is returned, certified-mail service has failed. 
If certified mail fails, the case is not dismissed automatically, and the user does not need to start over. Your options are switch to another service method quickly. Common next options include personal service by an adult who is not part of the case, sheriff service, or a registered process server.  
The user should also watch the service deadline. The defendant usually must be served at least 15 days before the hearing if they are in the same county, or at least 20 days before the hearing if they are outside the county. Certified mail failing does not restart that deadline
If there is not enough time to complete service before the hearing, the user should ask the court to postpone the hearing. In California small claims court, this is done with Form SC-150, Request to Postpone Trial. It is located in our optional forms section. The request generally should be filed at least 10 days before the hearing unless the user has a good reason for asking later. 
Recommended next step: check the case status with the clerk as soon as possible after the mailing attempt. If there is no signed receipt on file, do not wait. Use a more reliable service method and request a postponement if the hearing date is too close.

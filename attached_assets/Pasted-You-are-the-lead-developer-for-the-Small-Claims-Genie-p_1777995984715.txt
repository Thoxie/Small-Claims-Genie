You are the lead developer for the Small Claims Genie project.

Do not make any code changes yet.

Your task is to independently review the attached/source project and evaluate the issues identified below. Treat the prior review as an input, not as a final conclusion. Confirm, reject, refine, or expand each issue based on your own review of the codebase.

Please produce a written engineering/security assessment only.

Prior findings to evaluate:

1. Public/private object storage exposure
   The API appears to mount `storageRouter` before `requireAuth`, and `/storage/objects/*path` may have its auth/ACL logic commented out. Evaluate whether private uploaded evidence or generated documents could be accessed without authentication if a path is known or guessed.

2. Incomplete ownership checks on document update/delete
   The document routes appear to verify ownership of the case ID, but then update or delete documents by `docId` only. Evaluate whether a user who owns one case could update/delete a document belonging to another case if they know the document ID.

3. Missing ownership check on chat deletion
   `DELETE /cases/:id/chat` may delete chat messages for a case without first verifying the authenticated user owns that case. Confirm whether this is accurate.

4. Public debug/calibration/form tooling routes
   Some SC-100 debug, calibration, verification, evaluation, and preview endpoints may be publicly reachable. Evaluate whether these routes could expose internal tooling, consume OpenAI/API resources, mutate calibration files, or otherwise be unsafe in production.

5. Public upload URL route
   `POST /storage/uploads/request-url` may issue signed upload URLs without authentication. Evaluate whether this is used, whether it is protected elsewhere, and whether it creates an abuse risk.

6. Stale or broken `/sc100` frontend page
   The standalone SC-100 generator page may not attach Clerk auth headers to protected API calls and may send an intake body shape that no longer matches the backend schema. Evaluate whether this page is still used, broken, or obsolete.

7. “Repeat verbatim” chat shortcut
   The chat route may include a dev shortcut where a message containing “repeat verbatim” returns raw case context and OCR/document text. Evaluate whether this is a production risk and whether it should be removed or gated.

8. Non-atomic AI rate limiter
   The AI rate limiter may read the current counter and then update it separately, allowing concurrent requests to exceed limits. Evaluate whether this matters at expected scale and whether a transactional or atomic DB update is needed.

9. Uploaded OCR/document text placed into AI context
   The app appears to place OCR text directly into AI context. Evaluate whether prompt-injection boundaries are sufficient and whether uploaded documents should be treated explicitly as untrusted evidence rather than instructions.

10. Heavy/generated/archive artifacts in repository/package
    The source package may include nested zip files, large mockup files, generated artifacts, or calibration materials that should not ship. Identify whether these should remain in the repo, move to docs/artifacts, or be removed.
